# Project 006 — Role & Persona Prompting

## Date

2026-07-22

## Category

Prompt Engineering

## Difficulty

Intermediate

## Focus

Role Prompting, Persona Design, Prompt Optimization

---

# Objective

Evaluate how assigning a specific role or persona to an AI model influences the quality, tone, and relevance of its responses. Compare a generic prompt with a role-based prompt and analyze the differences.

---

# Task

Provide advice to a startup founder preparing to pitch investors.

---

# Prompt A

> Give advice for pitching investors.

---

# Prompt B

> You are a venture capitalist with 15 years of experience investing in early-stage technology startups.

> A first-time founder is preparing for a 10-minute investor pitch.

Provide practical advice covering:

- Pitch structure
- Common mistakes
- Financial metrics investors expect
- Delivery tips
- Questions investors may ask

Use a professional but encouraging tone.

Present the response using clear headings and bullet points.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Role Definition | Is the assigned role clear and relevant? |
| Context | Does the prompt provide enough background? |
| Specificity | Are expectations clearly defined? |
| Output Quality | Is the expected response likely to be useful? |
| Consistency | Will the prompt produce reliable outputs? |
| Overall Quality | Overall effectiveness of the prompt |

---

# Prompt Analysis

## Prompt A

### Strengths

- Very short.
- Easy to understand.
- Flexible enough for many types of responses.

### Weaknesses

- No expert perspective.
- No context about the founder.
- No response structure.
- May produce generic advice.
- Difficult to evaluate consistently.

---

## Prompt B

### Strengths

- Defines an expert role.
- Provides relevant context.
- Specifies required topics.
- Controls tone.
- Defines output structure.
- More likely to generate practical and actionable advice.

### Weaknesses

- Longer than Prompt A.
- Slightly reduces creative flexibility.

# Expected Output Comparison

## Prompt A

Likely characteristics:

- Generic advice
- Variable depth
- May miss important investor expectations
- Different structure across responses

---

## Prompt B

Likely characteristics:

- More practical recommendations
- Better organized
- Investor-focused insights
- Higher consistency
- Easier to compare across AI models

---

# Recommendation

Prompt B is more effective because it provides the AI with a clear role, relevant context, and specific expectations. These additions help generate responses that are more focused, actionable, and aligned with the user's goal.

While Prompt A allows for greater flexibility, it lacks the guidance needed to consistently produce high-quality outputs.

# Reflection

## What I Learned

Assigning a role or persona helps guide the AI toward a specific perspective and communication style. Combined with clear context and structured instructions, role prompting can significantly improve the relevance and consistency of generated responses.

---

## What I'd Do Differently

Experiment with different expert personas, such as an angel investor, startup founder, or accelerator mentor, to compare how the assigned role influences the quality and focus of the AI's advice.
